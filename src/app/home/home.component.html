The Constitution of India is the supreme law of the country, laying the foundation for its democratic governance and legal framework. It was adopted on November 26, 1949, and came into effect on January 26, 1950. The document establishes the structure, powers, and duties of government institutions while safeguarding fundamental rights and freedoms for its citizens. It is one of the longest written constitutions in the world, consisting of a preamble, 470 articles, and 12 schedules. Drafted by the Constituent Assembly, with Dr. B.R. Ambedkar as its chief architect, the Constitution reflects the principles of justice, equality, liberty, and fraternity. It also provides for a federal system with a clear separation of powers between the executive, legislature, and judiciary, while ensuring the protection of cultural and regional diversity. The Indian Constitution has been amended several times to address the evolving needs of the nation, yet it remains a living document, representing the aspirations of a sovereign, socialist, secular, and democratic republic.

The Supreme Court of India is the highest judicial authority in the country, serving as the ultimate guardian of the Constitution and the final court of appeal. Established on January 28, 1950, it functions as the apex court in India's judicial system, ensuring justice, upholding the rule of law, and protecting the rights and freedoms of citizens. The court has the power of judicial review, allowing it to interpret the Constitution and strike down laws or government actions that violate constitutional provisions. The Supreme Court also hears significant cases related to constitutional interpretation, civil and criminal matters, and disputes between states or between the central and state governments. It consists of the Chief Justice of India and a sanctioned strength of up to 33 other judges, all of whom are appointed by the President of India. The court plays a pivotal role in maintaining the balance of power among the executive, legislature, and judiciary, and is widely regarded as a crucial pillar in safeguarding democracy and justice in India.
